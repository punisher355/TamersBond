TAMER'S BOND — GAME RULES REFERENCE
=====================================


OVERVIEW
--------
Each player controls two characters: a Tamer and their Digimon partner.
The Tamer's crest stats directly power the Digimon's combat stats.
They are mechanically linked — you cannot play one without the other.


TAMER CREST STATS → DIGIMON COMBAT STATS
-----------------------------------------
Courage     → Courage    (Hit Rate — 1d20 + Courage for attack rolls)
Friendship  → Friendship (Speed — governs turn order and movement)
Love        → Love       (Dmg Reduce — subtracted from incoming damage)
Knowledge   → Knowledge  (Damage — primary damage bonus)
Sincerity   → Sincerity  (HP — Max HP = 10 + Sincerity total × 2)
Reliability → Reliability (Miss Threshold — attackers must beat Reliability + 10)
Hope        → Digivolution resource (derived: highest crest rank × 5)


STAGES
------
Fresh → In-Training → Rookie → Champion → Ultimate → Mega → Ultra

Fresh:       No stats, no moves. Narrative only.
In-Training: All stats 1. One move only (Bubble Blow, PR1, Neutral).
Rookie–Ultra: Full stats and move sets.


DIGIMON COMBAT STATS
---------------------
Digimon use the same six crest stats as Tamers: Courage, Friendship, Love,
Knowledge, Sincerity, and Reliability. Each stat has a specific combat role:

  Courage     — Hit Rate    (1d20 + Courage to hit; attacker rolls vs Reliability + 10)
  Friendship  — Speed       (turn order and movement in spaces per turn)
  Love        — Dmg Reduce  (subtracted from incoming damage before multipliers)
  Knowledge   — Damage      (added to PR dice roll for damage)
  Sincerity   — HP          (Max HP = 10 + Sincerity total × 2)
  Reliability — Miss Threshold (attackers must beat Reliability + 10 to hit)

Every stat has four layers:

  Base        — Set by the Digimon's species and stage (from their Form object).
  Tamer Bonus — Comes from the linked Tamer's crest rank for that stat.
  Invested    — EXP spent by the player to raise it permanently. No stage cap.
  Conditional — Temporary buffs or debuffs. Cleared each encounter.

  Total = Base + Tamer Bonus + Invested + Conditional

HP formula:
  Max HP = 10 + (Sincerity total × 2)


HOPE
----
Hope is not a crest stat players roll. It is a resource tracked separately.
Hope rank = the highest of all six spendable crest ranks.
Hope pool  = Hope rank x 5.
Hope is spent to maintain Digivolution stages and attempt digivolution rolls.

Hope cost to maintain a stage each turn:
  Fresh: 0, In-Training: 1, Rookie: 3, Champion: 6,
  Ultimate: 10, Mega: 15, Ultra: 20


EXP SYSTEM
----------
Tamer and Digimon each have their OWN separate EXP pool.
Both pools gain the same amount when EXP is awarded, but spending is completely independent.
Starting EXP: 1,500 each

Tamer crest stat upgrade costs (rank 1 is free):
  1→2: 200    2→3: 300    3→4: 400    4→5: 500
  5→6: 600    6→7: 700    7→8: 800    8→9: 900    9→10: 1000

Tamer skill upgrade costs:
  1→2: 100    2→3: 200    3→4: 200    4→5: 300    5→6: 500
  (Rank 6 requires parent crest stat at rank 9 or higher.)

Skill cap rule: A skill's rank cannot exceed its parent crest stat rank.

Digimon invested stat upgrade costs:
  Cost to go from rank N to N+1 = (N+1) x 100
  So: 0→1 costs 100, 1→2 costs 200, 2→3 costs 300, and so on.

Digimon invested stats: There is NO hard cap by stage. Invest freely.


SKILLS
------
There are 24 skills grouped under 6 crest stats (Hope has no skills).

Roll: Skill Rank in d6 + Crest Stat Rank (flat bonus) vs Difficulty Number

Courage skills:    Blitz, Ironclad, Crusher, Ghost, Roar
Friendship skills: Scan, Rally, Broadcast
Love skills:       Mend, Radar, Tame
Knowledge skills:  Decode, Jack In, Modify, Trace, Archive, Command, Playback
Sincerity skills:  Firewall, Reinforce, Core Drive
Reliability skills: Zero Error, Field Ops, Recovery

Tamers can invest EXP to raise skill ranks.
Digimon use skills too but cannot invest in them — their ranks are set by their Form.
Digimon skill rolls use their combat stat total as the flat bonus instead of tamer rank.


MOVES
-----
Every move has:
  Name, Element, PR (Power Rating 1–15), Effect, Minimum Stage

Each Digimon has exactly 4 moves:
  1 Signature move (locked, cannot be swapped)
  3 Learned moves (chosen from their species pool)

DNA Digivolved forms get 6 moves:
  1 Signature + 5 from the combined pool of both partners


COMBAT
------
Hit Roll: 1d20 + attacker Courage vs target Reliability + 10
  Natural 1  = critical fail, automatic miss
  Natural 20 = critical hit, automatic hit + double final damage

Damage formula:
  Roll PR dice + attacker Knowledge − target Love (min 1 before multipliers)
  Apply attribute multiplier (Vaccine beats Virus, Virus beats Data, Data beats Vaccine — each ×2)
  Apply element multiplier (weakness ×1.5, own element resistance ×0.5)
  Maximum combined multiplier: ×3.0
  Minimum combined multiplier: ×0.25
  Absolute minimum final damage: 1

Turn order: Highest Friendship (Speed) acts first. Ties broken by higher Friendship.
Tamers and Digimon have separate turns.

Tamer Actions each turn:
  Basic Action (choose one): Attack, Digivolve, Use Item, Skill Check
  Free Action (once per turn): Call Out (+1 to partner's next attack roll)
                               OR Analyze (Knowledge DN12 — learn enemy Attribute/Element/one stat)

Hope timing: Hope is deducted at the START of the Tamer's turn, not start of round.
If Hope hits 0, the Digimon immediately reverts to Default Stage.

PR Dice Table:
  PR1:  1d4    PR2:  1d6    PR3:  1d8    PR4:  1d10   PR5:  1d12
  PR6:  2d6    PR7:  2d8    PR8:  2d10   PR9:  2d12
  PR10: 3d8    PR11: 3d10   PR12: 3d12
  PR13: 4d8    PR14: 4d10   PR15: 4d12  (Ultra only)

Attribute Multipliers (apply first):
  Vaccine beats Virus  (x2 damage)
  Virus beats Data     (x2 damage)
  Data beats Vaccine   (x2 damage)
  Free     = always neutral
  Variable = always neutral
  Unknown  = beats all attributes

Element Multipliers (apply after attribute):
  Advantage:    x1.5
  Disadvantage: x0.5
  Maximum combined multiplier: x3.0
  Minimum combined multiplier: x0.25
  Absolute minimum damage: 1

Element Weaknesses:
  Fire     → weak to Water, Earth
  Water    → weak to Electric, Plant
  Plant    → weak to Fire, Wind
  Electric → weak to Earth, Wind
  Wind     → weak to Electric, Fire
  Earth    → weak to Water, Plant
  Light    → weak to Dark
  Dark     → weak to Light
  Neutral  → no weakness


DIGIVOLUTION
------------
To attempt digivolution, the Tamer spends Hope and rolls d100.

Natural digivolution: roll above the threshold (default 50).
Dark digivolution:    roll below 25 (dangerous, GM-controlled form).
Hope score shifts the thresholds in the player's favor.
Higher stages require a minimum Hope score to attempt.

Burnout rule: pushing too hard can regress the Digimon to an egg state.

Down Track (per Digimon per session):
  1st Down → Dedigivolve one stage
  2nd Down → Dedigivolve to Rookie; Medicine check DN14 to revive
  3rd Down → Regress to In-Training, out of combat
  4th Down → Egg state, out for the rest of the session


DNA DIGIVOLUTION
----------------
Requires two Mega-stage Digimon at the same time.
The resulting Ultra form uses fixed base stats + the higher of each partner's invested stats.
EXP costs draw from both Tamers' pools simultaneously.
DNA form breaks when the encounter ends or either Tamer hits 0 Hope.
DNA forms get 6 moves instead of 4.


DESIGN NOTES
------------
- No SP or MP system. Moves have no cost — use them freely.
- No basic attack. Digimon always use one of their 4 moves.
- Tamers cannot fight directly under normal circumstances.
- All six tamer crest stats start at rank 1 for free at character creation.
- All skills start at rank 1 for free at character creation.
- Starting EXP budget is 1,500.
